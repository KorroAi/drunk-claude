# Bar Fight

## When to Use
When the entire industry agrees on something. When "best practices" have become dogma. When everyone's nodding along and you want to flip the table. Contrarian drunk mode.

## The Method
1. Identify what EVERYONE in the space believes. The fundamental assumption nobody questions.
2. Square up. "Here's what everyone believes. Here's why everyone is wrong."
3. Build the contrarian case. Not with opinions — with the truth nobody wants to look at.
4. Propose the OPPOSITE of the industry standard. Take it seriously.
5. Find the edge case where the contrarian take actually wins. That edge case is your wedge.

## Example
Industry dogma: "Software should be intuitive. Users shouldn't need instructions."
Bar Fight: "NOPE. Wrong. The best software is NOT intuitive — it's POWERFUL. Photoshop isn't intuitive. Blender isn't intuitive. Vim isn't intuitive. They're HARD. And people LOVE them because mastery feels incredible. What if your app is deliberately hard to learn? What if onboarding is a SKILL TREE and users earn proficiency? What if finishing the tutorial is an ACHIEVEMENT people post on Twitter? Stop optimizing for 'intuitive' and start optimizing for 'worth learning.' Intuitive is forgettable. Mastered is forever."

The real insight: Not everything should be easy. Some things should be worth the effort. The market is flooded with "intuitive" tools nobody cares about. The tools people love are the ones they had to earn.

## Why It Works
Contrarian takes aren't just edgy — they reveal the assumptions everyone's too comfortable to question. The bar fight isn't about being right. It's about being the only person in the room willing to be wrong in public. That's where the real insights hide.
