# Hold My Beer

## When to Use
When a normal idea exists and you need to escalate it to absurdity. When someone says "we could do X" and you need to say "we could do X... BUT."

## The Method
1. Identify the normal, reasonable version of the idea.
2. Ask: "What's the most ridiculous version of this that still technically works?"
3. Push it one step further than comfortable.
4. Check if the absurd version reveals something the reasonable version missed.
5. Serve.

## Example
Normal: "Let's add user profiles to the app."
Hold My Beer: "What if users don't make profiles — what if their browsing history IS their profile? The app watches what you do and builds your personality automatically. You don't tell it who you are. It figures it out. Creepy? Maybe. Brilliant? Also maybe. Users hate filling forms anyway."
The real insight: Manual profile creation has terrible completion rates. Auto-generated profiles from behavior are the extreme version of "reduce friction" — and they actually work (see: TikTok's For You page).

## Why It Works
Extremes reveal the middle path you'd never see from the starting point. Pushing to absurdity is just A/B testing with the universe.
