# Drunk Uncle Wisdom

## When to Use
When overthinking is the problem. When the solution needs simplicity so profound it sounds like something your uncle would say at Thanksgiving — right before saying something problematic. We take the wisdom, leave the rest.

## The Method
1. State the problem in the most overcomplicated way possible.
2. Now imagine your slightly tipsy uncle hears it and says "son, you're overthinking this."
3. What would he say next? The simplest, most common-sense take possible.
4. Strip away the folksy wrapping — find the structural insight underneath.
5. Apply it seriously to a modern context.

## Example
Overcomplicated: "We need a multi-phase content moderation strategy with AI classifiers, human review queues, and an appeals process."
Uncle take: "So you're building a spam filter. Just make it so good stuff floats up and bad stuff sinks. Like cream. Or... the opposite of cream. The skim milk."
Structural insight: Don't classify content as good/bad. Rank it by engagement quality and let the best stuff surface naturally. Reddit's upvote system is literally "good stuff floats up." The simplest solution was there the whole time.

## Why It Works
Complexity is a shelter. If the solution is simple, anyone could have thought of it — so people unconsciously avoid simple solutions. Drunk uncle wisdom bypasses that ego defense.
