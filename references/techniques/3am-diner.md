# 3AM Diner

## When to Use
When you've been thinking about something too long. When organized brainstorming failed. When you need the kind of idea that only comes after midnight at a Waffle House.

## The Method
1. Dump everything you know about the problem in a stream. No structure. No paragraphs. Just let it flow.
2. Don't judge. Don't organize. The mess is the point.
3. Look at the dump and find the one weird thing you said that you didn't plan to say.
4. That weird thing — the tangent, the aside, the thing in parentheses — that's your idea.
5. Build on it. The tangent was the main road all along.

## Example
Problem: "Our onboarding flow has a 60% drop-off rate."
3AM dump: "ok so users land on the page they see a signup form with 8 fields obviously they leave because who wants to fill out 8 fields for an app they haven't even tried yet what if we just let them use the app first and ask questions later actually what if the app doesn't even have a signup button you just start using it and at some point it's like 'hey by the way if you want to save this you should make an account' and by that point they're already invested so they will..."
The tangent that became the idea: "what if the app doesn't even have a signup button." That's the whole product rethink right there.

## Why It Works
Your brain's first ideas are the safe ones. The fifth or sixth thing you say — when you're too tired to filter — that's where the real ideas live. Stream of consciousness excavates them.
